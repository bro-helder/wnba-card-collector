WNBA Team Gradient System

Every card in Chase is rendered with a team-centric gradient — primary color flowing into secondary, grounded to near-black. This doc captures the recipe for all 15 active franchises (including 2026 expansion teams Toronto Tempo and Portland Fire) so card art stays consistent whether a collector pulls a Fever rookie or a Valkyries 1/1.

Authored in response to Brook's comment on Caitlin Clark IND RC #1. Updates here flow directly into data.js → window.TEAMS and re-render every card.

Version 1.1 Teams 15 (all active WNBA) Updated for 2026 season · +TOR, +POR

01Gradient Anatomy

Every team gradient follows the same three-stop recipe. Only the colors and angle change per team.

Formula
linear-gradient({angle}, {primary} 0%, {secondary} 60%, #0A0A0B 100%)
Stop 1
Primary team color — full saturation, sets the team's visual identity at the top of the card.
Stop 2
Secondary team color at 60% — creates the mid-card transition and adds a secondary brand cue.
Stop 3
Chase black (#0A0A0B) at 100% — grounds the card, gives the nameplate a legible base, and ties every team card to the same visual language.
Angle
145°–160°. Default 155°. Diagonal flow makes the card feel like it has direction. Teams with horizontal wordmarks (GSV, MIN) skew to 145°; teams with tall marks (LVA, PHX) skew to 160°.
Parallel overrides
Base / Silver uses the straight team gradient. Mojo, 1/1, Gold Vinyl, and tinted Ice parallels replace stops 1–2 with the parallel's own palette but keep the team's secondary as a tint layer.

03Usage Rules

How to stay consistent when adding new teams, parallels, or treatments.

Do

Always ground in black

Stop 3 is non-negotiable. Chase black unifies every team card and keeps the nameplate legible at the bottom. Never drop it even if the team has no dark brand color.

Do

Use official primary/secondary

Pull hex values from the team's current brand guidelines, not NBA-affiliated colors. Sparks use LA purple/gold (sister team), but Sky is its own Chicago palette — not Bulls red.

Careful

Light primaries need contrast

Liberty (seafoam) and Fever (gold) have light primaries. The monogram and player name must use dark text (#0A0A0B) on the hero mark area, or a strong text shadow — not white on white.

Careful

Some teams have low internal contrast

Phoenix's sunburst-orange-to-deep-purple and Valkyries' gold-to-navy are high-contrast. Seattle green-to-yellow reads cleanly. Aces gold-to-black, Washington red-to-navy, and Liberty green-to-black need the mid-stop handled carefully or they read as one block.

Don't

Don't invent team colors

When future expansion teams join (Cleveland 2028, Detroit 2029, Philadelphia 2030), wait for official brand guidelines before adding. Document the source URL in a comment on window.TEAMS.

Don't

Don't stack more than 3 stops

More stops make the card noisy and compete with parallel treatments. If a team has three brand colors (e.g. Storm green + orange + yellow), pick the two that define the jersey — keep the third for accents elsewhere.
